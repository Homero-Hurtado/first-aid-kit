Alka-Seltzer

Antacid

Indications

1. Place an Alka-Seltzer tablet in a glass of water.
2. Wait for the tablet to completely dissolve in the water.
3. Mix if necessary.
4. Drink the effervescent solution once the tablet has completely dissolved.
5. You may experience quick relief from symptoms after taking it.

Aloe vera

Burns

Indications

1. Wash the burn with cold water to reduce skin temperature and relieve pain, dry it gently.
2. Apply the gel directly to the burn gently.
3. Apply Aloe vera several times a day, as needed. Regular application can help relieve pain and speed up the healing process.
4. Cover the burn with a sterile bandage or gauze after applying Aloe vera.

Antibacterial gel

Sanitizer

Indications

1. Apply a sufficient amount of antibacterial gel to the palm of one hand.
2. Rub the gel into your hands, making sure to cover all surfaces: palms, backs of hands, between fingers, fingers, and areas around wrists.
3. Continue rubbing your hands together until the gel has completely evaporated and your hands are dry.

Antiseptic towels

Skin cleansing

Indications

1. First clean the area of skin to be treated with soap and water before using the antiseptic towel.
2. Gently rub the antiseptic towel over surface to be disinfected.
3. Keep the surface wet with the antiseptic for the time recommended on the label.
4. Once you have cleaned and disinfected the surface with the antiseptic towel, allow it to air dry.
5. Do not reuse

Applicators

Cleaner/Applicator

Indications

1. Before using the applicator, make sure the area is clean and ready for the procedure. If necessary, clean the skin or surface with soap and water and dry properly.
2. Dip the end of the applicator into the solution until it is properly soaked.
3. apply it gently to the skin, rub to clean a wound, or use it to perform other specific tasks.
4. Throw away after use

Aspirin

Fever and headache

Indications

1. Follow specific directions based on the patient's age, weight and condition.
2. Take the aspirin with water. Avoid taking it with other liquids such as juice, milk or alcohol, as they may affect its absorption and increase the risk of side effects.
3. Do not use aspirin for a long period of time without consulting a doctor.

Band aids

Protective film

Indications

1. Clean the wound with mild soap and water, and pat dry with a clean towel.
2. Choose a band-aid that fits the size and shape of the wound.
3. Carefully remove the protective paper from the band-aid, avoiding touching the adhesive part with your fingers to keep it sterile.
4. Apply the band-aid directly to the wound, making sure it adheres firmly to the skin around the wound.

Bandages

Reinforcing

Indications

1. Make sure the area to be bandaged is clean and dry.
2. Place one end of the bandage on the area to be bandaged and holding it firmly.
3. Wrap around the area to be bandaged, making sure to completely cover the wound or injured area.
4. Secure the final end with a clip, tape, or by tying a soft knot.

Blood pressure cuff

Pressure meter

Indications

1. Sit comfortably and rest your arm on a flat surface with your palm facing up.
2. Place the bracelet around your bare arm, approximately at heart level and secure the bracelet firmly.
3. Turn on the meter and wait for it to initialize and press the start button to start measurement.
4. The cuff will begin to inflate automatically. Look at the screen to get your blood pressure results

Calamine

Itching and skin irritation

Indications

1. Clean and dry affected skin before applying.
2. Shake the calamine jar or bottle well before using it.
3. Apply a thin, even layer of calamine to the affected skin using a clean applicator, cotton ball, or your clean fingers.
4. Let the calamine air dry completely before covering the area with clothing or bandages.

Cotton

Cleaner/Applicator

Indications

1. Make sure the area to be treated is clean and dry.
2. Unravel or unfold the cotton gently to obtain an even surface.
3. Apply liquid products or solutions to the cotton until it is saturated, avoiding excessive drips.
4. Be sure to handle the cotton with care and gentleness, especially on sensitive or wounded areas.
5. Once you have used a piece of cotton, dispose of it properly.

DesenfriolD

Anti-flu

Indications

1. Follow the manufacturer's instructions and follow directions for appropriate dosage based on patient's age, weight and condition.
2. Take Desenfriol-D with water. Avoid another drinks, as these may affect absorption and increase the risk of side effects.
3. If multiple doses are necessary, follow the manufacturer's recommendations or your health care professional's advice.

Ethyl alcohol

Disinfectant

Indications

1. Cleanse skin with mild soap and water then moisten a piece of cotton or sterile gauze with ethyl alcohol.
2. Gently rub the area in circular motions, making sure to cover the entire surface to be disinfected. Let the ethyl alcohol sit on the skin for at least 30 seconds.
3. Allow the ethyl alcohol to evaporate completely before covering the disinfected area.

Face mask

Breathing protector

Indications

1. Before putting on or taking off a face covering, wash your hands with soap and water or use an alcohol-based hand sanitizer.
2. Hold the mask by the ear flaps or straps, avoiding touching the front.
3. Adjust the mask to the shape of your face, making sure it completely covers your nose, mouth and chin.
4. Remove the mask by the ear flaps or straps, avoiding touching the front.

Fexofenadina

Allergies

Indications

1. Follow the manufacturer's instructions and follow directions for appropriate dosage based on patient's age, weight and condition.
2. Take Desenfriol-D with water. Avoid another drinks, as these may affect absorption and increase the risk of side effects.
3. If multiple doses are necessary, follow the manufacturer's recommendations or your health care professional's advice.

Gauze

Liquid absorption

Indications

1. Before applying a gauze pad, clean and dry the affected area.
2. Place the gauze directly over the affected area. Secure the gauze in place using medical tape, bandages, or elastic bandages.
3. Avoid applying the gauze too tightly.
4. Change the gauze as needed, usually whenever it gets wet, dirty, or at least once a day to keep the area clean and protected.

Glucometer

Blood glucose meter

Indications

1. Insert a test strip into the glucometer port.
2. Adjust the depth of the lancet as needed and use the lancet to obtain a drop of blood from a finger.
3. Wipe away the first drop of blood and wait for another drop of blood to flow to perform the test.
4. Touch the end of the test strip to the drop of blood until the sample is absorbed by the strip.
5. Discard the used test strip.

Hydrocortisone

Skin conditions

Indications

1. Wash and dry the affected area before using the medication.
2. Wait for the tablet to completely dissolve in the water.
3. Gently rub the medication into your skin until it is completely absorbed.
4. Avoid contact of hydrocortisone with the eyes, mouth, mucous membranes, and other sensitive areas of the body.
5. Wash your hands after applying.

Hydrogen peroxide

Disinfectant

Indications

1. Carefully clean the wound with mild soap and water.
2. Soak a piece of cotton or gauze with hydrogen peroxide and gently apply to the affected area.
3. Let the hydrogen peroxide act for a few seconds to allow disinfection.
4. Rinse the wound or skin area with clean water to remove any hydrogen peroxide residue.
5. Gently dry the skin area with a clean towel or tissue.

Ibuprofen

Pain and fever

Indications

1. Take ibuprofen with food or milk to reduce the risk of gastrointestinal side effects.
2. Follow your doctor's recommendations or medication package to determine the proper dosage and frequency of administration.
3. Avoid exceeding the maximum recommended daily dose and do not take ibuprofen for long periods without consulting a doctor.

Latex gloves

Hand protector

Indications

1. Wash your hands and make sure your hands are completely dry before putting on the gloves.
2. Hold the glove by the rolled edge or wrist, insert your fingers into the glove.
3. Carefully slide the first glove over your hand, making sure it fits properly over your fingers, palm, and wrist.
4. Make sure they are snug and completely cover your hands.

Lomotil

Diarrhea

Indications

Lomotil is used to treat acute and chronic diarrhea in adults.
1. Take Lomotil with water.
2. If you experience stomach pain or heartburn, you can take Lomotil with food or milk to reduce discomfort.
3. Follow your doctor's recommendations or the instructions on the medication package to determine the proper dosage and frequency of administration.

Metamucil

Constipation

Indications

1. Mix Metamucil with at least 8 ounces (240 ml) of water or your favorite beverage and stir the mixture well to make sure it is completely dissolved before consuming.
2. Drink the mixture immediately after preparing it.
3. Be sure to drink enough fluids throughout the day while taking Metamucil to prevent dehydration and facilitate bowel function.

Paracetamol

Pain and fever

Indications

Paracetamol is used to treat mild to moderate pain, such as headaches, muscle aches, toothaches, back pain, and pain associated with conditions such as arthritis and It is also used to reduce fever associated with colds, flu and other infections.
1. Take paracetamol with a full glass of water.
You can take paracetamol with or without food, depending on your preferences and needs.

Surgical forceps

Tweezers

Indications

1. Select the appropriate surgical forceps for the specific procedure.
2. Make sure the surgical forceps are clean, disinfected and sterilized.
3. Handle surgical forceps with care and precision to avoid damaging tissue, causing injury, or compromising the integrity of the materials.
4. After use, clean and disinfect the tweezers.

Syringe

Medication injection

Indications

1. Remove the syringe from its packaging and verify that it is clean, sterile and in good condition.
2. Prepare or dilute the medication.
3. Extract the medication from its container using the syringe and prevent the formation of air bubbles.
4. Clean and disinfect the administration site with an appropriate antiseptic.
5. Administer the medication carefully.

Tape

Bra

Indications

1. Clean and disinfect the skin to ensure optimal adhesion and prevent contamination or infection.
2. Make sure the skin is dry before applying the tape to avoid premature peeling or lack of adhesion.
3. Cut medical tape to the proper size and shape for the specific application.
4. Apply the tape carefully and firmly.

Thermometer

Temperature measurement

Indications

1. Make sure the thermometer is clean, disinfected and in good condition.
2. Prepare and calibrate the thermometer to ensure accurate and reliable measurements.
3. Place the thermometer according to the selected method (e.g. under the tongue, in the armpit, in the rectum, in the ear) and ensure a stable and safe position.

Vaseline

Lubricant/Moisturizer

Indications

1. Wash and dry the skin area or surface where the Vaseline will be applied to ensure a clean and effective application..
2. WApply a thin, even layer of Vaseline to skin, dry, cracked or irritated areas, or as directed and needed.
3. Gently massage the Vaseline into the skin or affected area to facilitate absorption and improve the effectiveness of the product.

Vick syrup

Dry and phlegm cough

Indications

1. Follow the dosage and administration indicated on the product packaging or according to the doctor.
2. Use the dispenser provided to measure and administer the proper dose of cough syrup.
3. Consult a doctor or health care professional before giving cough syrups to children under a certain age or to people with specific medical conditions.
